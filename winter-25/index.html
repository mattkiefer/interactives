2024 was the warmest year on record.

By Matt Kiefer

Last year was the hottest in recorded history on planet Earth, according to early data collected by the Copernicus Climate Change Service.

In Chicago, the average temperature was 55° Fahrenheit at O'Hare International Airport — 3.8° above normal.

Source: National Weather Service

The Washington Post reported at least 359 primary climate sites tracked by the Southeast Regional Climate Center saw record-high temps last year, including the following cities:

  • San Jose
  • Phoenix
  • Atlanta
